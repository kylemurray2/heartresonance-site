The practitioner problem

You know HRV biofeedback works. The friction is everything around it.

01

Expensive proprietary sensors create a barrier

A client motivated enough to buy a $129 sensor after their first session is one in ten. The other nine drop out between week one and week three — the exact window the research tells us is critical for establishing a practice. A $30 chest strap they already own (or easily buy) removes that friction entirely.

02

Opaque coherence scores make it hard to coach

When a client reports "I got 75% coherence today," you can't tell whether they found their resonance, whether their peak was dominant, or whether they just happened to have a good vagal day. The six criteria give you something to actually discuss.

03

Session data is trapped in the vendor's cloud

You can't feed it to your clinic's assessment workflow. You can't look at it across a program. You can't even export it for the client's records. Heart Resonance exports every session as a plain CSV — bring it into your own tooling.

04

Subscriptions compete with your own fees

Clients pushing back on $10/month for an app they barely understand is a common reason the practice stops. A one-time unlock removes that recurring decision. They pay once, have the tool forever, and their compliance becomes a clinical question rather than a billing one.
